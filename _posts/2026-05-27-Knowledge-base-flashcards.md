Another thought on an extension to Andrej Karpathy's knowledge base. My discussions with LLMs on research papers and textbook study is important as a mechanism for exploration and analysis, as well as documenting the discourse. LLM can then distill those discussions into the knowledge base of "what did I learn?", as a snapshot of what I learned.

The "how do I remember" practice and rehearsal step for retention is still missing. To make this a full learning loop, one mechanism is flashcards generated from the knowledge base. Of course the LLM would generate these, but the process of reviewing them is something I think I will still rely on current workflow Anki for - the UI works for me (i.e., on my phone), it is convenient, and it's already built and battle-tested. I can leverage work being done for FlashGen to implement this as well and restart my ML learning flash cards decks.

An extension to FlashGen would be a *free-form* flashcard generator which bypasses the intermediate Japanese audio flashcard generator and would allow the LLM to create any flashcard in Anki, perhaps using AnkiConnect directly.
